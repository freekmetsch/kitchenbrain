Direction A · Recommended

Kitchen Timeline

A quiet counter tray leads into a generous chronological timeline. Stable colored rails carry streams through every numbered step and combine at merges.

Main strength

The strongest balance of scanability, stream meaning, and low-friction counter use.

Trade-off: It still feels like a purposeful application rather than a printed recipe.

Review this direction

Direction B

Mise-en-Place Board

Counter readiness is the visual anchor. Ingredients are grouped by semantic stream before the denser numbered execution list begins.

Main strength

Best for gathering ingredients, making substitutions, and seeing parallel recipe parts before work starts.

Trade-off: Ingredient-heavy recipes can create a dense opening before step one.

Review this direction

Direction C

Cook's Notebook

The cooking plan reads like a carefully edited recipe. Stream color becomes annotation rather than structure, and the counter tray folds into the page.

Main strength

The calmest and most editorial reading experience, with almost no dashboard feeling.

Trade-off: Counter state, swaps, and parallel streams require more deliberate attention.
